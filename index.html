About me

Bio

I was born in Colombia and moved to the United States in 2004 at the age of nine. Growing up, I enjoyed life in California but sought a change, so at 21, I decided to return to Colombia. While living there, I worked in a call center and pursued a degree in business administration. Midway through my studies, I discovered a passion for software engineering and made the decision to transition into the field. I returned to the U.S., obtained a software engineering certification, and later enrolled in college to earn an associate degree in Computer Information Systems, all while gaining valuable experience working as a waiter in a Japanese restaurant.

Hobbies

From a young age, I developed a strong passion for sports, particularly bodybuilding and mixed martial arts. In high school, I wrestled before transitioning to bodybuilding, where I had the privilege of training with industry icon CT Fletcher. Upon moving back to Colombia, I reignited my passion for MMA, eventually competing in a national amateur tournament and securing first place.

Beyond sports, I have a deep appreciation for traveling and cultural exploration. I have lived in Brazil and Mexico for several months and traveled throughout Western Europe. Additionally, I have a strong interest in reading, particularly biographies and history books. I enjoy trying to understanding how past societies functioned and their similarities to modern life.

My love for technology

While renting an Airbnb in Colombia I met a senior software engineer who introduced me to the world of technology. As he shared insights about his work and the tech industry’s culture, I found it far more intriguing than my current path in business. His passion and experiences ultimately inspired me to make the switch to software engineering.

Determined to pursue this new direction, I enrolled in a rigorous coding academy in California, where I quickly realized the challenges of learning to code. Although the journey was more demanding than I had anticipated, my passion for technology continued to grow. Over time, I became certain that software engineering was not just a career choice but a lifelong pursuit.
